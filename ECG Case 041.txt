ECG ANSWER and INTERPRETATION
This is an ECG example of dilated cardiomyopathy demonstrating signs of enlargement of all four cardiac chambers:
*There is marked LVH with very deep S waves in V2-4. https://litfl.com/left-ventricular-hypertrophy-lvh-ecg-library/
*Right axis deviation suggests associated RV enlargement (= biventricular enlargement). https://litfl.com/right-axis-deviation-rad-ecg-library/
*Evidence of left atrial enlargement (deep, wide terminal portion of the P wave in V1). https://litfl.com/left-atrial-enlargement-ecg-library/
*Peaked P waves in lead II suggestive of right atrial enlargement (~ 2.5mm in height). https://litfl.com/right-atrial-enlargement-ecg-library/
This patient had four-chamber dilatation on echocardiography with severe congestive cardiac failure (awaiting cardiac transplantation).
